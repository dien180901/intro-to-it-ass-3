LECTURER: MR. NGUYEN MINH LONG

3. Career plan 

Dien Ma 

Firstly, in my university life, i want to build my computer science foundation for my future plan. Now, i am on the right track on our plan, i am finding a software engineer job to gain more experiences about software industry. After graduating from RMIT, i will try to pursuit a master degree in Computer Science at Standford. Then, i will start my bussiness about education. 

Now i am in the final step of interview process to get a junior software engineer job in Orange Logic. If i can pass this interview and get a position in this compony, i will try to work 2 year for this company to have a working experience which can meet the requirement for any master degree. I Chose software engineer is my dream job especially in web development.  

Honestly, my career plan can be challenging for me, but i will try my best to pursuit this dream 

 

Thanh Nguyen 

First and foremost, while working on our project and after listening to each member's narrative, I have realized that I need to make some minor adjustments to my career path. I chose data scientist as my dream career from the very beginning of the course, in the very first assignment, without providing any specifics on how I would go about obtaining it. As a result, I will choose a backend internship as my first step since it is extremely difficult to get a data internship without asking for prior expertise or knowledge. I am on my path to building my CV by acquiring new skills such as Javascript, HTML, CSS, and Nodejs. After that, I would want to put together a few modest online projects to attach with my CV. 

Dien and Tam have certain components in their ideal jobs that are similar to mine, such as web development and backend programming. However, Dien wants to be a software engineer. He seeks jobs as a fresher or a junior software engineer with his knowledge and confidence. In terms of job aspirations, Tam is the one who is comparable to mine since he hopes to become a full-stack developer in the future. He is also the one who advises on my future IT plan. With My, my opinion is that her ideal job would be very different from the rest of us since her dream career does not mention anything about the IT area. 

Finally, with the help and guidance of my teammates, I was able to clear a small portion of my career path by understanding what I needed to study and prepare for my CV. Compared to my friends, I am the only one who has no precise intentions regarding salary or ideal companies; instead, I would want to constantly do the best that I can so that I do not have to look back and regret my decisions in the past. Those are compelling arguments in support of my belief that I am not required to limit myself to any specific item since anything might happen. The day you want a position in a specific company, there might not be there waiting for you to apply after your graduation. Your dream salary may be as high as you want for now, but no one can predict whether the price will still be the same in the future. 

My Nguyen 

In short term goal, within 5 years after my graduation, I would like to gain more practical knowledge and industry intelligent by either working for a company or continuing my further study. If I choose to apply for a company, I will prefer a business analytics position or business consulting. Otherwise, I could apply for Master study in Australia. In fact, in January 2022, at the writing time of this report, I passed the CV round and the first interview at KPMG (a company well-known as BIG 4 which is the top 4 global largest auditing firms). Despite having few more rounds ahead including the test round and the group interview round, I am quite confident until now. Thus, if I could get the position, I might work in this company around 1 to 2 years before starting my post graduate study.  

Regarding my long-term career, becoming a global entrepreneur having influence on young start-up is my dream. In other words, after 5 years to gain experience, I would like to set up my own business. 

Overall, my career plan at first might sound not really related to IT world, but in fact today business is strongly disruptive my state-of-the-art technologies. This is to say without the knowledge of technological things, the higher chance that a person will be unemployed and the higher chance the start-up might fail. In short, I believe that having knowledge and expertise in IT field is essentially supportive to my career plan. 

Tam Kieu 

My dream is to become a Full Stack Software Engineer. So, in the early stage of my IT journey, I want to have an internship offer as a Java developer at the end of my first year. Currently I’m working hard on the Backend language and knowledge such as Java, OOP, Data structure, Algorithm... I also take 3 hours a day to schedule a Frontend self-study plan about JavaScript and React/ReactJS. To be honest, I’m working on seeking a company for an intern position now. I ‘ve made some project about Java and some websites with CSS, HTML and JS attached in my CV. I also try to solve at least one problem a day on Leet Code to improve my logic and coding skills. 

For the long-term, I want to work in Europe for 2 years, then I move to Singapore for 2 years as a Full stack Developer. Basically, 10000$ per month is aimed at my goal for the future salary after 4 years since graduation. And the last thing I want to achieve is owning a Software solution company. 

For overall, my career plan seems not too special or big, but It’s not easy to achieve. I used to be a student in Business field before switch to IT, therefore, to achieve my goals I must work as harder as possible. And one big question is how I can make my previous major’s knowledge useful. I believe there’s nothing useless, maybe I begin later than the others, but my previous time might be a competitive advantage for my IT career path.  

Group comparison 

It is obvious that the three males in group all finds the common hobby in sport activities while My is more into leadership and academic activities. Regarding future expected jobs, half of the team members are passionate in being developers, both front-end and full stack, including Dien and Thanh while the other two people (My and Tam) share a common interested jobs in business intelligent analyst where technology and business fields are blended to address social needs to create and capture values. It is also noticeable that although Tam has interest in full stack developer, but his long-term goal is to adopt technological advancement as a tool to support his business pathway more efficiently. This is proved in his craving for either a business intelligent analyst or an expected technology start-up entrepreneur. More detail about the plan to pursue and achieve the job goals, our team members have drawn up the comprehensive plans which are SMART (specific, measurable, attainable, relevant, and time-bound). However, in this section it is not the main purpose to repeatedly explain what those are but comparing to highlight the similarities and differences between our plans are conducted. More specifically, all of the members expect to have more self-learning, self-practice and internship to be equipped with sufficient practical knowledge. Dien aims to graduate from RMIT with high GPA to increase his employability compared to others while My builds a plan to follower higher study (Post-graduate) in Australia.
